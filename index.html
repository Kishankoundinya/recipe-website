Gulab Jamun

Gulab jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from milk solids, flour, and sugar syrup. To prepare it, mix 1 cup of milk powder, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, 1/4 teaspoon of baking soda, and 2 tablespoons of ghee in a bowl. Gradually add milk (about 2-3 tablespoons) to form a soft dough. Avoid over-kneading to ensure the gulab jamuns remain soft. Divide the dough into small, smooth balls without cracks. Meanwhile, heat oil or ghee in a deep pan on medium-low heat for frying. Fry the balls until golden brown, ensuring they cook evenly by gently stirring. For the syrup, boil 2 cups of sugar with 1.5 cups of water, a few cardamom pods, and a splash of rose water or a pinch of saffron for added aroma. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until slightly sticky but not thick. Soak the fried balls in the warm sugar syrup for at least an hour, allowing them to absorb the sweetness. Serve warm or at room temperature for a delicious treat.

Gajar halwa

Gajar halwa, a beloved Indian dessert, is made by slow-cooking grated carrots with milk, sugar, and ghee. Begin by washing and peeling 1 kg of fresh carrots, then grating them finely. Heat 4-5 cups of full-fat milk in a large, heavy-bottomed pan, and bring it to a boil. Add the grated carrots and cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the milk is absorbed and the mixture thickens. Stir frequently to prevent burning. Once the milk has reduced, add 1/2 cup of sugar (adjust to taste) and continue to cook, allowing the sugar to dissolve completely. Stir in 4 tablespoons of ghee and cook until the mixture turns glossy and aromatic. Finally, add 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder and a handful of chopped nuts like almonds, cashews, and raisins for flavor and texture. Cook for a few more minutes, then serve warm, garnished with more nuts if desired. This rich and aromatic dessert is perfect for celebrations or as a comforting treat.

Rasmalai

Rasmalai is a delightful Indian dessert featuring soft cottage cheese patties soaked in flavored milk. To make it, begin by boiling 1 liter of full-fat milk in a pan. Once it boils, reduce the heat and gradually add 2 tablespoons of lemon juice or vinegar, stirring until the milk curdles and separates into whey and paneer. Strain the paneer using a muslin cloth, rinse under cold water to remove acidity, and hang it for 30 minutes to drain excess water. Knead the paneer into a smooth, soft dough and shape it into small, flat discs.

In another pan, prepare a sugar syrup by boiling 1 cup of sugar with 4 cups of water. Add the paneer discs and cook on medium heat for about 15 minutes until they double in size. Meanwhile, prepare the flavored milk (rabri) by boiling 1 liter of milk until it thickens slightly. Add 1/4 cup of sugar, a pinch of saffron strands, and 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder. Stir well and let it simmer.

Once the paneer discs are cooked, gently squeeze out excess syrup and transfer them to the warm rabri. Chill the rasmalai in the refrigerator for a few hours, then serve garnished with chopped pistachios and almonds for a rich, creamy treat.

Kaju Barfi

Kaju Barfi, or cashew fudge, is a rich and popular Indian sweet made with cashews, sugar, and ghee. To prepare it, begin by grinding 2 cups of cashews into a fine powder using a blender. Be careful not to over-blend to avoid releasing the cashew oil. In a non-stick pan, dissolve 3/4 cup of sugar in 1/4 cup of water over medium heat, stirring until you achieve a one-string consistency syrup. Lower the heat and gradually add the cashew powder, mixing continuously to avoid lumps.

Cook the mixture on low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens into a smooth dough-like consistency. Add a teaspoon of ghee and mix well. Transfer the mixture onto a greased parchment paper or a plate. Let it cool slightly, then knead it gently while itâ€™s warm to ensure a smooth texture.

Roll the dough evenly into a flat sheet about 1/4-inch thick, using another sheet of parchment paper on top to prevent sticking. Optionally, apply edible silver leaf (varak) for decoration. Allow it to cool completely, then cut into diamond-shaped pieces. Your rich and creamy kaju barfi is ready to enjoy or share during festive occasions!

Rose rasgulla

Rose rasgulla is a delightful variation of the classic Indian dessert, featuring soft cottage cheese balls infused with the essence of rose. To prepare, start by boiling 1 liter of full-fat milk in a pan. Once it boils, reduce the heat and gradually add 2 tablespoons of lemon juice or vinegar, stirring until the milk curdles and separates into whey and paneer. Strain the paneer using a muslin cloth, rinse under cold water to remove acidity, and hang it for 30 minutes to remove excess water.

Knead the paneer until smooth and pliable, then divide it into small portions and roll them into smooth, crack-free balls. In a large pot, prepare sugar syrup by boiling 1 cup of sugar with 4 cups of water. Once the sugar dissolves, add 1-2 tablespoons of rose water and a few drops of pink food coloring (optional) to give it a rosy hue.

Gently drop the paneer balls into the boiling syrup, cover, and cook on medium heat for about 15 minutes. The rasgullas will double in size as they cook. Once done, let them cool in the syrup. Chill the rose rasgullas in the refrigerator for a few hours, then serve garnished with dried rose petals or pistachios for an aromatic and beautiful dessert.
